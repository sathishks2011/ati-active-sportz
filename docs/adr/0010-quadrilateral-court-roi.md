# Court ROI is a quadrilateral, not a rectangle

**Status:** Accepted (2026-05-21).

The Court ROI is represented as four user-tapped corner points (normalized 0..1, ordered top-left â†’ top-right â†’ bottom-right â†’ bottom-left in screen space), not a screen-aligned rectangle. Setup adds an explicit "tap the four corners of the court" step in the Variant-B guided wizard (decisions-log: "Setup screen design direction"). Both detection Modes from ADR-0009 (`'motion'` and `'players'`) treat "inside the Court ROI" as "inside the user-tapped polygon" â€” the motion worklet applies a point-in-quad mask in its sampling loop, and the future person-detector worklet filters detections by centroid-in-quad.

This supersedes the rectangle aspect of the existing decisions-log entry "Court ROI: per-Session, required, not persisted." The per-Session and not-persisted aspects of that entry are unchanged â€” the polygon is still drawn fresh every Session and not carried across.

Reason: a volleyball court in any side-view or end-view camera shot is a trapezoid, never a screen-aligned rectangle. The previous rectangle ROI was forced to either over-include (coaches pacing the sideline, scorers' table, sideline traffic, court corners of the *adjacent* court) or under-include (chops the corners of the active court, particularly on cross-court plays). Informal field testing surfaced this leakage as the primary source of false positives â€” motion outside the actual playing surface tripping the trigger. A polygon ROI is the smallest change that closes the geometric leak without introducing ML and improves both Modes equally, which keeps the A/B comparison between Modes honest: only the player-count gate differs, not the ROI shape.

A second reason â€” load-bearing for the `'players'` Mode and any future both-sides-of-net check â€” is that four labelled corners give us a homography from screen coords to canonical-court coords (a unit rectangle whose y-axis runs from one baseline to the other, split at 0.5 by the net). With a homography in hand, the person detector's "is this person on the court?" test is a precise point-in-polygon check, and a future "rally requires players on both sides of the net" gate is a free trivial extension (split mapped-y at 0.5). Neither becomes possible from a screen-aligned rectangle without also asking the user to tell us where the net is.

Considered alternatives:
- **Keep the rectangle, tighten the threshold or shrink the rect manually.** Rejected: doesn't fix the geometric mismatch â€” sideline activity is *inside* even a tight rect in any non-overhead camera view. Also throws away part of the actual court.
- **Polygon with more than four vertices (user traces the court outline).** Rejected: four corners is the natural shape of a volleyball court and a one-tap-per-corner UX. More vertices add UX friction without adding signal â€” the court has four corners, no more.
- **Auto-detect the court via line detection / segmentation.** Rejected for MVP: another CV problem to debug across gyms, and adds a startup latency that conflicts with the Warm-up budget from ADR-0006. Worth revisiting in Phase 2 as a UX nicety, not as a substitute for user-tapped corners.
- **Two-step setup: rect (existing) + optional polygon refinement.** Rejected: keeps a rect codepath alive that nobody benefits from once polygon is shipped. YAGNI.

Consequences:
- **Setup UI gains a four-corner-taps step.** The Variant-B guided wizard's Step 1 splits into 1a (frame the court with optional pinch-to-zoom, decisions-log: "Pinch-to-zoom at Setup") and 1b (tap the four court corners in TLâ†’TRâ†’BRâ†’BL order). Step 2 (live-preview with dim-outside-ROI) is unchanged in role but renders a polygon dim, not a rect.
- **Roi shape changes in code.** `Roi` in `src/state/sessionMachine.ts` is `{corners: [[x, y], [x, y], [x, y], [x, y]]}` (normalized, ordered TLâ†’TRâ†’BRâ†’BL). All consumers (motion worklet, overlays, persistence) compile against this shape.
- **Motion worklet adds a point-in-quad mask.** The sampling loop in `motionFrameProcessor.ts` iterates the polygon's axis-aligned bounding box (so we don't pay for samples that can't possibly contribute) and applies a four-cross-product point-in-quad test per sampled pixel. Cheap â€” fewer than 10 additional float ops per sample, and the bounding-box optimization means the sample count is bounded above by today's rect-ROI cost.
- **Adaptive baseline scope shrinks accordingly.** The Warm-up baseline (ADR-0006) is accumulated only at sample positions that fall inside the polygon. This means the per-sample baseline arrays in the worklet are sized by "samples that pass the mask," not "samples in the bounding box." Behaviourally this is what we want â€” idle-noise calibration on the actual court surface, not on the dead space outside it.
- **Persistence schema migrates.** `sessions.roi_x/y/w/h` â†’ `sessions.roi_corners` (JSON, four `[x, y]` pairs). One-step migration; no in-flight Sessions in production yet.
- **Overlay components change shape.** `CourtRoiOverlay` and `Dimmer` render a polygon path (SVG or four-segment View) rather than a rect. Inside `Dimmer`, the "outside" region is the screen minus the polygon â€” usually rendered as a single SVG mask path.
- **Homography utility lands separately.** A small helper that turns the four corners + screen dimensions into a 3Ă—3 matrix mapping screen-coords to canonical-court-coords is *not* required for the open-Segment gate, only for the future both-sides-of-net check. Ship it when first needed.
- **CONTEXT.md glossary updates.** The Court ROI definition is amended from "rectangular region" to "quadrilateral region defined by four user-tapped corners," with the per-Session and not-persisted aspects called out unchanged.

Hard to reverse (touches Setup UI, persistence schema, motion worklet, and the overlay components; reverting means undoing all four); surprising-without-context (a future engineer reading "why does the user tap four corners?" deserves the geometric-leak rationale, especially given the original decisions-log entry explicitly chose rect for friction reasons); the result of a real trade-off (rect was deliberately chosen as one-drag UX, polygon trades one tap of friction for closing the leak and enabling the future homography).
